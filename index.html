HEAT STROKE


Heatstroke is the most severe type of heat-related illness with a core body temperature above 104°F (40°C). It is a condition which results from the inadequate function of the body's heat-regulating mechanism, often as a result of prolonged exposure to high temperatures. Signs may be confusion, palpitation, nausea, and sometimes fainting. Heat stroke is a medical emergency and early treatment helps to avoid such complications as organ damage or fatal outcomes.
